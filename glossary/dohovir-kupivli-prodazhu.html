Dohovir kupivli-prodazhu (Purchase Agreement)

The notarially authenticated real estate purchase agreement required for property transfers in Ukraine, registered in the State Register of Rights to Immovable Property.

A dohovir kupivli-prodazhu (contract of purchase and sale) is the primary legal document for transferring real estate ownership in Ukraine. Ukrainian law requires that property purchase agreements for residential and commercial real estate be notarially authenticated by a licensed Ukrainian notary (notarius) or, in some cases, by a state notary at a state notary office. After authentication, the notary registers the ownership transfer directly in the Unified State Register of Rights to Immovable Property (Derzhavnyi reestr rechovykh prav). This electronic registration is what legally transfers ownership.

The Ukrainian real estate registration system underwent significant modernisation in the 2010s, transitioning from paper-based land books to a centralised electronic register. The Unified State Register of Rights (Derzhavnyi reestr) now records all property rights including ownership (pravo vlasnosti), mortgages (ipoteka), and other encumbrances. The register is accessible online at the Ministry of Justice portal (kap.minjust.gov.ua), allowing anyone to check current ownership and encumbrances by property address or cadastral number. This transparency is a significant improvement over older systems and enables efficient due diligence.

Before signing any purchase agreement, obtain a current extract from both the Unified State Register of Rights and the State Land Cadastre (Derzhavnyi zemelnyi kadastr) if land is involved. The extract will confirm ownership details and any registered encumbrances. Your Ukrainian notary (notarius) will typically obtain these extracts as part of the pre-signing verification process. However, given the current wartime context in Ukraine, additional caution is warranted, and independent verification through your own avokat (lawyer) is strongly recommended.

Property taxation in Ukraine is relatively light by European standards. There is no capital gains tax on the sale of a primary residence held for more than 3 years. Property transfer tax (derzhavne myto) and pension fund levy are charged at low rates. Income tax may apply to investment property sales. Rental income from Ukrainian property is subject to income tax for residents and a specific rate for non-residents. The tax landscape has been subject to changes related to wartime fiscal measures, and consulting a Ukrainian tax adviser is essential for accurate current guidance.

The security situation in Ukraine since 2022 has significantly affected the real estate market and practical ability to complete transactions. Property in frontline regions, occupied territories, or areas with military infrastructure has obvious risks. For foreigners and diaspora Ukrainians considering purchases, consulting with a Ukrainian lawyer (advokat) with current local knowledge is essential. Some property transactions have been suspended, valuations are highly volatile, and physical access to properties may be restricted. Despite these challenges, some areas of Ukraine — particularly western cities like Lviv — continue to have active property markets.

Key Facts

  • Notarial authentication mandatory; notary registers ownership in state electronic register
  • Unified State Register accessible online at Ministry of Justice portal
  • Both buyer and seller must provide personal identification codes (RNOCPP)
  • Low property transfer taxes compared to Western Europe
  • Current wartime situation creates significant additional risks for foreign buyers

Common Mistake

Foreign buyers sometimes proceed without obtaining a Ukrainian tax identification code (RNOCPP/IPN) before the signing, causing last-minute delays. Every buyer — including foreign nationals — must have a Ukrainian tax identification number to complete a property registration. Apply for this code at a Ukrainian tax office or embassy well in advance of your planned purchase. Without it, the notary cannot complete the registration.

Practical Tip

For any Ukrainian property purchase in the current environment, engage an independent avokat (lawyer) separate from the one recommended by the estate agent. The lawyer should conduct searches of court records (ЄДРСР portal) for any litigation involving the property or seller, and check the Unified Register of Debtors for any enforcement proceedings against the seller. These searches are fast and cheap but can reveal serious issues invisible in the property register alone.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can foreigners buy property in Ukraine?

Yes, foreign nationals can purchase residential and commercial property in Ukraine. Since 2022, some restrictions have been introduced for nationals of countries designated as aggressor states. Agricultural land purchases by foreigners remain restricted under the land reform law. All foreign buyers need a Ukrainian tax identification code (RNOCPP) to complete a registration.

How is property ownership registered in Ukraine?

The notary registers ownership directly in the Unified State Register of Rights to Immovable Property (Derzhavnyi reestr rechovykh prav) electronically at the time of the notarial signing. You receive a printout extract confirming your registration as owner. The registration is immediate and does not require a separate filing period.

What are the risks of buying property in Ukraine currently?

Risks include physical damage from conflict, title complications in occupied or disputed territories, market volatility, currency risk (Ukrainian hryvnia), enforcement and access difficulties, and regulatory changes related to wartime legislation. Properties in western Ukraine away from active conflict zones carry lower risk, but comprehensive legal due diligence and insurance assessment are essential.
