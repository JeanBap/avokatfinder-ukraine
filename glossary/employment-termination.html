Employment Termination

Legal procedures and protections governing the ending of employment relationships, including notice requirements and severance obligations.

Definition

Employment termination in Ukraine is strictly regulated to protect workers from arbitrary discharge and to ensure fair procedures for ending employment relationships. Employment can be terminated by mutual agreement, by the employer with just cause, by the employer with advance notice, or by the employee resigning. The circumstances under which termination is allowed and the procedures that must be followed are specified in the Labor Code and applicable employment laws.

Termination for just cause occurs when an employee engages in serious misconduct such as theft, violence, gross negligence, or repeated violations of employment duties despite warnings. Termination for cause does not require advance notice but must follow disciplinary procedures including investigation, notice of charges, and opportunity for the employee to respond. Documentation of the misconduct must be thorough, as improper dismissal can be challenged.

Termination without cause by the employer requires advance notice and often severance pay. The notice period varies depending on employment length and is specified in the employment contract or by law. During the notice period, the employee is entitled to time off to seek alternative employment. Severance pay is typically based on length of service and last wages. Failure to provide proper notice or severance can result in liability for wages and damages.

Employees have the right to resign by providing notice to the employer. The resignation notice period is typically shorter than the notice period required for employer termination. Resignation should be in writing and should include the date the resignation takes effect. Upon resignation, the employee is typically entitled to payment of accrued wages and benefits.

Unlawful termination occurs when the employer terminates employment in violation of legal requirements. Unlawfully terminated employees can seek reinstatement, back pay, damages for lost earnings, and compensation for harm to reputation. Labor courts review termination decisions to ensure compliance with legal requirements. Any termination decision should be carefully documented and should comply with statutory procedures.

Key Facts

  • Employment termination requires following statutory procedures and providing notice and/or severance.
  • Termination for cause requires investigation and opportunity for employee to respond.
  • Termination without cause requires advance notice and severance pay based on length of service.
  • Employees have the right to resign with advance notice.
  • Unlawful termination results in reinstatement, back pay, and damages.

Common Mistake

Employers terminate employees without proper documentation or notice, exposing themselves to wrongful termination claims.

Expert Tip

Document performance issues and follow disciplinary procedures before terminating for cause to reduce exposure to wrongful termination claims.
