Ipoteka (Іпотека)

A mortgage or real estate security right in Ukraine

Ipoteka is a real property security right in Ukraine whereby a lender holds a priority claim over a specific property to secure repayment of a loan. Regulated by the Law of Ukraine on Mortgage (Law No. 898-IV), the mortgage must be notarised and registered in the State Register to be effective against third parties.

Ukrainian mortgage lending is supervised by the National Bank of Ukraine (NBU). Since 2022, government mortgage programmes — particularly the government-subsidised 'eOselyia' programme — have supported homebuying for Ukrainian citizens, including military personnel and certain displaced persons.

How It Works in Practice

Mortgage interest rates in Ukraine have historically been high by European standards, but the eOselyia programme offers government-subsidised rates. Buyers eligible for the programme should explore it before committing to a commercial mortgage.

The registration of a mortgage in the State Register creates a publicly visible encumbrance on the property. Buyers checking the property register for encumbrances will see the mortgage listed. Any buyer purchasing a mortgaged property must ensure the mortgage is discharged (note pysmennoho vykluchennya) simultaneously with the ownership transfer.

Key Facts

Legal BasisLaw of Ukraine on Mortgage (Law No. 898-IV)
RegulatorNational Bank of Ukraine (NBU)
Government ProgrammeeOselyia — subsidised mortgages for eligible Ukrainians
Register VisibilityEncumbrance visible in State Register to any searcher
EnforcementCourt or out-of-court procedure per mortgage agreement

Mortgage enforcement in Ukraine follows the Law on Mortgage and requires either a court judgment or, where the mortgage agreement permits, an out-of-court enforcement procedure. Due to the ongoing conflict, mortgage enforcement has faced disruptions in some areas, and lenders have applied various forbearance measures.

Common Mistake: Buyers sometimes purchase property without checking the register for mortgage encumbrances. A property with an unresolved mortgage cannot be cleanly registered in the buyer's name — the notary will flag the encumbrance and require its discharge.
Expert Tip: If eligible for the eOselyia government mortgage programme, apply before finalising the purchase agreement. The programme's eligibility criteria and subsidised rate can significantly improve affordability compared to commercial alternatives.

Frequently Asked Questions

What law governs mortgages in Ukraine?

Mortgages in Ukraine are governed by the Law of Ukraine on Mortgage (Law No. 898-IV) and related National Bank of Ukraine regulations.

What is the eOselyia programme?

It is a government-subsidised mortgage programme in Ukraine offering reduced interest rates for eligible citizens, including military personnel, teachers, and medical workers.

How is a mortgage encumbrance discharged in Ukraine?

After full loan repayment, the lender provides a discharge confirmation, and the notary removes the encumbrance from the State Register simultaneously with or following the final payment.
