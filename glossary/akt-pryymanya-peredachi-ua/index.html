Akt Pryymanya-Peredachi (Акт приймання-передачі)

The transfer and acceptance deed documenting property handover in Ukraine

Akt pryymanya-peredachi is the formal document recording the physical transfer of a property from seller to buyer in Ukraine. Signed at the moment of possession handover, it confirms that the buyer has accepted the property in a specific condition and that the seller has vacated.

The deed records the condition of all rooms, fixtures, and systems at the time of handover, utility meter readings for settling outstanding bills, any items included in the sale (furniture, appliances), and the keys and access codes handed over. It is the evidentiary foundation for any post-handover dispute about the property's condition.

How It Works in Practice

Under Ukrainian civil law, the risk of accidental damage or destruction passes from seller to buyer at the moment of handover recorded in the akt. If the property suffers accidental damage between the contract signing and the akt, the seller bears the loss unless the contract provides otherwise.

The akt is particularly important for new-build apartment purchases (novobud), where it records defects in construction quality that the developer must remedy. Buyers of new builds should inspect thoroughly and note all visible defects in the akt before signing — after signing, the burden shifts to the buyer to prove pre-existing defects.

In Ukraine's war context, the akt pryymanya-peredachi for any property should also note the condition of windows, roof, and structural elements with particular care, as war-related damage may be present but not immediately obvious during a brief inspection.

Common Mistake: New-build buyers sometimes sign the akt pryymanya-peredachi at the developer's urging without thoroughly inspecting the apartment. Defects noted in the akt obligate the developer to remedy them; defects missed at handover become the buyer's problem.
Expert Tip: Bring a checklist and a torch to the handover inspection. Test every socket, switch, tap, and door. Check window seals and note any cracks in walls or ceilings. A thorough akt signed the same day is far more valuable than a complaint filed weeks later.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the akt pryymanya-peredachi signed?

It is signed at the moment of physical handover — when the seller gives the buyer the keys and the buyer takes possession of the property.

Does the akt need to be notarised?

No. The akt is not required to be notarised. It is a standard written document signed by both parties, each retaining an original.

What if the developer refuses to let me note defects in the akt?

This is a significant red flag. You have the right to note observed defects. If the developer refuses, do not sign the akt and document the refusal in writing. Seek legal advice before proceeding.
