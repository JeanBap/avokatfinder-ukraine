Derzhavnyy Reyestr Rechovykh Prav (Державний реєстр речових прав)

Ukraine's State Register of Real Property Rights — the authoritative ownership database

Derzhavnyy reyestr rechovykh prav na nerukhome mayne (State Register of Real Property Rights) is Ukraine's central electronic register of all property rights, including ownership, mortgages, easements, and long-term leases. Managed by the Ministry of Justice, it is the authoritative source for determining who owns a property and what encumbrances affect it.

The register was established in 2013 and became the sole authoritative source for property rights information, replacing the previous paper-based BTI (Bureau of Technical Inventory) registration system. Historical rights registered with the BTI remain valid but should be re-registered in the new electronic system when the property is next transacted.

How It Works in Practice

Buyers can access the register through the Ministry of Justice's online portal (drv.minjust.gov.ua) to obtain an extract (informatsiyna dovidka) confirming the current owner, any encumbrances, and recent transactions. Online extracts are available to anyone and are generated in minutes.

Registration is performed by notaries and state registrars simultaneously with (or immediately after) the execution of the relevant legal document. The electronic system generates a registration number and timestamp, creating an immutable record of when each right was registered.

War RiskRecords in conflict-affected areas may need enhanced verification

In war-affected areas, some property records may have been compromised, destroyed, or fraudulently altered. Ukraine has implemented special measures to protect and recover property records in such areas, but buyers of property near or formerly in conflict zones should conduct enhanced document verification.

Common Mistake: Buyers sometimes rely on a paper ownership certificate from the old BTI system without checking the current electronic register. The register is the authoritative source — always request a current extract, as the paper certificate may not reflect recent encumbrances or transactions.
Expert Tip: Run a register extract immediately before the notary appointment — not just during initial due diligence. A fresh extract on the day of signing confirms no last-minute encumbrances have been registered since your initial check.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the derzhavnyy reyestr rechovykh prav?

It is Ukraine's central electronic register of all real property rights, managed by the Ministry of Justice. It is the authoritative source for ownership and encumbrance information.

Can anyone access the property register in Ukraine?

Yes. The register is publicly accessible online at drv.minjust.gov.ua. Anyone can obtain an information extract for any property.

Are old BTI paper certificates still valid?

Yes, but they should be updated in the electronic register when the property is next transacted. The electronic register is now the authoritative system.
