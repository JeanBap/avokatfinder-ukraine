Podatky na Prodazh Nerukhomosti (Податки на продаж нерухомості)

Taxes payable on real estate sales in Ukraine

Selling real estate in Ukraine may trigger several taxes: personal income tax (PIT / PDFO) on the gain, military levy (viyskovyy zbir), and state duty (derzhavne myto). The applicable taxes depend on the seller's residency status, the holding period, and how many properties the seller has sold in the current tax year.

For Ukrainian tax residents: the sale of residential property held for at least three years (or the seller's primary home) is generally exempt from PIT and military levy. The second or subsequent sale within a calendar year, or sale of a property held less than three years, is subject to 5% PIT plus 1.5% military levy on the declared sale price.

How It Works in Practice

Non-residents selling Ukrainian real estate pay 18% PIT plus 1.5% military levy on the sale price, regardless of holding period. Double tax treaty provisions may reduce the effective rate for residents of treaty countries — professional advice is essential for non-resident sellers.

State duty (derzhavne myto) is payable on the notarisation of the purchase contract. The rate is 1% of the declared sale price, typically split between buyer and seller (0.5% each), plus the Pension Fund contribution of 1% payable by the buyer.

Key Facts

Resident ExemptionPrimary home or 3+ year hold — PIT and levy exempt
Taxable SalesSecond sale in year or sub-3-year hold: 5% PIT + 1.5% levy
Non-Resident Rate18% PIT + 1.5% levy on full price
State Duty1% of price (typically 50/50 buyer/seller); 1% Pension Fund (buyer)
Under-DeclarationCriminal offence; notary coordinates payment verification

All taxes connected with a real estate sale are coordinated through the notary at the signing appointment. The notary collects the tax payments or confirms their prior payment before executing the contract. Under-declaring the sale price to reduce taxes is a criminal offence in Ukraine.

Common Mistake: Sellers sometimes forget that the military levy applies even where the PIT exemption does. A seller who has owned the property for three years and is selling their primary home is exempt from PIT — but in some periods the 1.5% military levy has still applied. Confirm the current rules with a tax adviser before pricing the property.
Expert Tip: Non-resident sellers should consult a Ukrainian tax lawyer before signing. The 18% PIT rate is very high, and applicable double tax treaties may reduce this significantly — but only if the correct procedures are followed before the notary appointment.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is a property sale exempt from income tax in Ukraine?

Residential property held for at least three years and sold as the seller's primary home is generally exempt from personal income tax and the military levy for Ukrainian tax residents.

What taxes does a non-resident seller pay?

18% personal income tax plus 1.5% military levy on the full declared price. Double tax treaties may reduce this — consult a specialist before the transaction.

What is the Pension Fund contribution on property purchases?

The buyer pays a contribution of 1% of the declared property price to the Pension Fund at the time of notarisation, in addition to other transaction taxes.
