Notarius (Нотаріус)

A licensed notary who certifies real estate documents in Ukraine

Notarius is a licensed public officer in Ukraine who authenticates legal documents, including real estate purchase contracts, mortgage agreements, powers of attorney, and inheritance certificates. Notarisation is mandatory for real estate transactions and simultaneously triggers property rights registration in the State Register.

Ukrainian notaries operate under the Law of Ukraine on Notariat and are supervised by the Ministry of Justice. There are two categories of notary: state notaries (derzhavni notariusy) and private notaries (pryvatni notariusy). Both have equal authority to certify real estate documents and perform registration.

How It Works in Practice

Since the 2016 reform, notaries have direct access to the State Register of Real Property Rights and register ownership simultaneously with certifying the purchase contract. This eliminates the separate registration step that previously added days or weeks to the transaction timeline.

Notary fees are regulated by the Law on Notariat and related ministerial orders. For real estate transactions, fees are typically calculated as a percentage of the declared transaction value, capped at certain amounts. Both parties usually share the notary fee, though the exact allocation is by agreement.

Choosing a reliable and experienced notary is critical in Ukraine. The notary is responsible for verifying that the property is encumbrance-free, that the seller has legal capacity and authority to sell, and that all taxes are paid before certifying the contract. A careless notary can expose the buyer to significant risks.

Common Mistake: Buyers sometimes choose a notary recommended by the seller's agent without independent verification. In Ukraine, where notary quality varies significantly, independently verifying the notary's standing with the Notary Chamber of Ukraine is a prudent step.
Expert Tip: Confirm with your chosen notary that they have current access to the State Register and will perform the ownership registration simultaneously with the contract signing. This is standard post-2016 but worth confirming explicitly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do both buyer and seller use the same notary in Ukraine?

Typically yes — both parties attend the same notary for the signing appointment, and the notary certifies the contract and registers the ownership transfer simultaneously.

What is the difference between state and private notaries in Ukraine?

Both have equal legal authority to certify real estate documents. Private notaries are more common and often more accessible; state notaries operate in state offices. The legal effect of notarisation is identical.

How do I verify a Ukrainian notary's standing?

The Notary Chamber of Ukraine (Notarialna Palata Ukrayiny) maintains a register of all licensed notaries. You can check a notary's licence status and any disciplinary history before engaging them.
