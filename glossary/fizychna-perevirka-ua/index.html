Fizychna Perevirka Neruhomosti (Фізична перевірка нерухомості)

Physical inspection and due diligence of a property before purchase in Ukraine

Fizychna perevirka neruhomosti refers to the physical inspection and technical due diligence of a property before completing a purchase in Ukraine. Given the ongoing conflict and the significant impact of the war on the Ukrainian property market and building stock, physical inspection is more important than ever.

A thorough physical inspection should assess the structural integrity of the building, the condition of all systems (electrical, plumbing, heating, ventilation), the state of common areas in apartment buildings, signs of water damage or mould, and the presence of any unpermitted alterations or additions.

How It Works in Practice

In conflict-affected areas, physical inspection must also assess war-related damage — shelling damage to the structure, broken windows, damaged roofs, and any impact on building services. Ukraine has developed a digital damage assessment system (DREAM) that records verified war damage to properties and can be cross-referenced during due diligence.

For properties outside conflict zones, standard due diligence applies. Buyers should commission an independent technical survey by a licensed building surveyor or engineer (inzhener-tekhnichnyy ekspert) rather than relying solely on their own visual inspection.

Transfer DeedAkt pryymanya-peredachi — document condition at handover

The condition of the property at the time of purchase should be documented in detail in the transfer deed (akt pryymanya-peredachi), signed at the moment of handover. Any defects noted in the deed provide a basis for compensation claims if they were not disclosed by the seller.

Common Mistake: Buyers sometimes skip a professional technical survey to save money. In Ukraine's current environment — where some properties have suffered war damage, and others have hidden defects exacerbated by wartime energy shortages — a professional survey is especially important.
Expert Tip: For any purchase in or near conflict-affected areas, cross-reference the property against Ukraine's DREAM damage database before viewing. This tells you whether any official war damage has been recorded, even if the visible condition appears normal.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the DREAM system?

DREAM is Ukraine's digital war damage assessment system that records officially verified war damage to properties. It can be used to check whether a property has registered damage before purchasing.

Should I commission a technical survey before buying in Ukraine?

Yes, particularly in the current environment. A licensed building surveyor can identify structural issues, systems failures, and war-related damage that may not be apparent from a visual inspection.

What is the akt pryymanya-peredachi?

It is the transfer deed signed at handover, documenting the physical condition of the property at the time of possession transfer. Defects noted in the deed provide a basis for compensation if not previously disclosed.
