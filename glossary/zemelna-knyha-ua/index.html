Derzhavnyy Zemelnyy Kadastr (Державний земельний кадастр)

Ukraine's State Land Cadastre for recording land parcel data

Derzhavnyy zemelnyy kadastr (State Land Cadastre) is Ukraine's official register of all land parcels, maintained by the State Geocadastre. It records the physical and technical characteristics of every land parcel: cadastral number, boundaries, area, land category (agricultural, residential, industrial, etc.), and permitted use.

The cadastre is distinct from the State Register of Real Property Rights. The cadastre records what the land is (physical facts); the register records who owns it and what rights exist over it (legal facts). Both must be checked during property due diligence, as they can diverge.

How It Works in Practice

Access to the State Land Cadastre is provided through the public cadastral map (publichnaya-kadastrovaya-karta.gov.ua), where anyone can view parcel boundaries, cadastral numbers, and basic land category information. Official extracts must be requested from the State Geocadastre for transaction purposes.

Land categories in Ukraine are strictly regulated. Changing a parcel's category — for example from agricultural to residential — requires a formal procedure with the relevant authority and can take significant time. Buyers planning to use land differently from its current category must investigate the feasibility of a category change before purchasing.

The State Land Cadastre has been progressively digitised, and the public map is a valuable tool for preliminary due diligence. However, the cadastre may not reflect very recent changes, and official certified extracts (vytiag z derzhavnoho zemelnoho kadastru) from the State Geocadastre are the authoritative documents for transactions.

Common Mistake: Buyers sometimes rely on the public cadastral map for due diligence without obtaining an official certified extract. The map is useful for orientation but is not an authoritative legal document — transactions require an official certified extract.
Expert Tip: Check the land category of any parcel you intend to purchase before making an offer. If the planned use is incompatible with the current category, investigate the time, cost, and probability of a successful category change before committing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the derzhavnyy zemelnyy kadastr?

It is Ukraine's State Land Cadastre, recording the physical characteristics of all land parcels — boundaries, areas, cadastral numbers, and land categories.

How is the cadastre different from the State Register of Real Property Rights?

The cadastre records physical facts about land parcels; the State Register records legal rights over them (ownership, mortgages, etc.). Both must be checked during due diligence.

Can I change the land category of a Ukrainian parcel?

Yes, but it requires a formal administrative procedure with the relevant authority, which can take considerable time. Investigate feasibility before purchasing land where a category change is needed.
