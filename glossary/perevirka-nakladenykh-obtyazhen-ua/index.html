Perevirka Naklaenykh Obtiazen (Перевірка накладених обтяжень)

Due diligence check for encumbrances and restrictions on Ukrainian property

Perevirka nakladenykh obtiazen refers to the due diligence process of checking all encumbrances, restrictions, and third-party rights registered against a property before completing a purchase in Ukraine. This check is essential to avoid inheriting hidden liabilities.

Encumbrances that can be registered in the State Register include mortgages (ipoteka), court-ordered prohibitions on alienation (zaborona vidchuzhenniya), tax liens, lease registrations, and easements. Any of these can prevent registration of a new owner or be enforceable against the buyer after purchase.

How It Works in Practice

The State Register can be searched online at drv.minjust.gov.ua. Buyers should run searches using the property's cadastral number, the seller's full name, and the seller's taxpayer identification number (РНОКПП) to catch any encumbrances registered under any of these identifiers.

In addition to the State Register, buyers should check for unpaid court judgments through the Unified State Register of Court Decisions (reyestr.court.gov.ua) and for tax debts through the State Tax Service's debtor register. These checks do not replace the State Register search but supplement it.

Search KeysCadastral number, seller name, seller РНОКПП
Supplementary ChecksCourt decisions register, tax debtor register
War CaveatCheck restricted zone status and damage for conflict-affected properties

For war-affected properties, additional checks may be required, including verifying whether the property is in a restricted zone, whether it has been damaged, and whether any state reconstruction or compensation programme affects the property. Ukraine's Ministry of Communities and Territories provides updated maps of affected areas.

Common Mistake: Buyers sometimes check the register only once at the start of due diligence and don't update the search before signing. A prohibition on alienation or new mortgage registered in the days before signing can block registration. Always run a fresh search on the morning of the signing appointment.
Expert Tip: Run searches using both the property's cadastral number and the seller's РНОКПП. A court-ordered prohibition may be registered under the seller's name rather than the property's number, and a name-only search would miss it.

Frequently Asked Questions

What types of encumbrances are registered in Ukraine's State Register?

Mortgages, court-ordered prohibitions on alienation, tax liens, registered long-term leases, and easements can all be registered as encumbrances.

How often should I check for encumbrances during due diligence?

At a minimum: once at the start of due diligence and again immediately before the signing appointment. For high-value transactions, additional intermediate checks are advisable.

Does a court judgment automatically register as a prohibition?

Courts can order a prohibition on alienation (zaborona vidchuzhenniya) which is then registered in the State Register. A judgment that has not yet been converted into a registered prohibition may not appear in the register but could be enforced after purchase.
