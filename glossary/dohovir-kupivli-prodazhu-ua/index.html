Dohovir Kupivli-Prodazhu (Договір купівлі-продажу)

The purchase and sale contract for real estate in Ukraine

Dohovir kupivli-prodazhu is the foundational legal instrument for transferring real estate ownership in Ukraine. The contract must be notarised by a licensed Ukrainian notary and registered in the State Register of Real Property Rights (Derzhavnyy Reyestr Rechovykh Prav) to complete the title transfer.

The contract must identify both parties with their taxpayer identification numbers (RНОКПП), describe the property precisely using its cadastral number, state the purchase price in Ukrainian hryvnias (UAH), and set out payment terms. The notary verifies the absence of encumbrances and confirms both parties' identities and consent.

How It Works in Practice

Since 2022, Ukraine has faced extraordinary challenges due to the ongoing war. Property transactions in conflict-affected or temporarily occupied territories are legally complex. Buyers are strongly advised to restrict purchases to areas of effective government control and to conduct enhanced due diligence on the property's history and physical condition.

The notary simultaneously registers the buyer's ownership right in the State Register during the signing appointment. This simultaneous registration system, introduced in 2016, means the buyer's title is secured on the same day as signing, reducing the window of vulnerability between contract and registration.

Transfer tax (prybutkovyy podatok and derzhavne myto) and notary fees are payable at the time of contract execution. The notary coordinates tax payment and ensures proof of payment is attached to the certified contract before submitting the registration request.

Common Mistake: Buyers sometimes execute contracts without verifying the property's tax debt status. In Ukraine, unpaid property taxes and utility debts can become a headache for the new owner even if they do not legally transfer. Request tax clearance certificates before signing.
Expert Tip: Use Ukraine's Ministry of Justice online services (reestr.court.gov.ua and drv.minjust.gov.ua) to pre-check the property's registered rights and encumbrances before the notary appointment. This takes minutes and can reveal critical issues before you sit down to sign.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does a purchase contract need to be notarised in Ukraine?

Yes. The contract must be notarised by a licensed Ukrainian notary. Without notarisation, the contract cannot be used to register ownership.

When does ownership transfer in Ukraine?

Ownership transfers on registration in the State Register of Real Property Rights, which in Ukraine typically happens simultaneously with notarisation on the day of signing.

Can foreigners buy property in Ukraine?

Yes. Foreigners can purchase apartments and non-agricultural real estate. Agricultural land purchase by foreigners is restricted. A taxpayer identification number (РНОКПП) is required for the transaction.
