Umova Orendi Neruhomosti (Договір оренди нерухомості)

A real estate lease agreement in Ukraine

Umova orendi neruhomosti is the lease agreement by which a landlord (orendodavets) grants a tenant (orendator) the right to use real property for a defined period in exchange for periodic rent (orenda) in Ukraine. The agreement is governed by the Civil Code of Ukraine and, for specific property types, by special laws.

Residential lease agreements in Ukraine are governed by Chapter 59 of the Civil Code and the Law on Rental of State and Municipal Housing. For private residential tenancies, the parties have significant freedom of contract, but minimum statutory protections apply — particularly regarding notice periods and the tenant's right to cure payment defaults before eviction.

How It Works in Practice

Commercial leases are governed by the Civil Code and subject to significant party autonomy. Key commercial lease terms include the rent amount (and indexation mechanism), lease term, permitted use, assignment and subletting rights, landlord and tenant maintenance obligations, and conditions for early termination.

Leases for a term of three years or more must be notarised and registered in the State Register of Real Property Rights to be enforceable against third parties, including a new owner who purchases the property. A buyer of a tenanted property should check the register for registered leases and obtain copies of all lease agreements from the seller.

War ProvisionsSpecial rules for conflict-affected areas — seek current advice

In areas affected by the ongoing conflict, Ukraine has enacted special rules on lease obligations — tenants in affected areas may be temporarily excused from rent obligations, and landlords have received some relief from mortgage and lease obligations. These wartime provisions are evolving and require current legal advice.

Common Mistake: Landlords of commercial property sometimes enter informal verbal agreements or short written agreements to avoid registration requirements. Unregistered leases are not enforceable against a new owner — a tenant in an unregistered long-term lease has no rights against a new purchaser.
Expert Tip: For any commercial lease, include a rent indexation clause tied to the consumer price index or foreign exchange rate, and specify what happens to rent during periods of force majeure. The war has made these provisions essential for any Ukrainian commercial lease.

Frequently Asked Questions

When must a Ukrainian lease be registered in the State Register?

Leases with a term of three years or more must be notarised and registered. Shorter leases can be unregistered but have limited enforceability against third parties.

What notice period is required to terminate a residential lease?

The Civil Code requires a minimum notice period for residential lease termination, but the specific period depends on the lease terms and the grounds for termination. Consult the current statutory rules, which may have been modified by wartime legislation.

Does a registered lease bind a new owner?

Yes. A lease that is properly registered in the State Register is enforceable against any subsequent buyer of the property for the remaining lease term.
