Buying Property in Ukraine as a Foreigner

12 min read  ·  Ukraine Legal Guide  ·  Updated April 2026

TL;DR — Quick Summary

Buying property in Ukraine as a foreigner is legal and relatively straightforward. Key costs: State duty: 1% (resident); 15% (non-resident); pension fund levy 1% transfer tax + approx. 0.5%–1% of property value notary fee. Register the transaction at the Державний реєстр речових прав (State Registry). Budget 8–12% of purchase price for total transaction costs.

Can Foreigners Buy Property in Ukraine?

Yes — there are no restrictions on foreign nationals purchasing property in Ukraine. In all cases, engaging a qualified local lawyer is strongly recommended before signing any document.

The Buying Process Step by Step

  1. Engage a lawyer — before viewing or making offers. Your lawyer conducts due diligence on the property, title, debts, planning status, and seller's ownership. This is non-negotiable.
  2. Obtain your local tax / identity number — required for any property transaction in Ukraine. Your lawyer can assist with this.
  3. Make an offer and sign a reservation agreement — typically a small deposit (1–5% of price) secures the property while due diligence is completed.
  4. Lawyer conducts title search — searches the Державний реєстр речових прав (State Registry) for encumbrances, mortgages, outstanding charges, planning violations, and ownership history.
  5. Sign the preliminary contract — binding agreement with completion date; typically 10% deposit payable here.
  6. Final deed before the Нотаріус (Notarius) — the Нотаріус (Notarius) authenticates the transfer of title. Both buyer and seller (or their legal representatives) must attend. Balance of purchase price is paid here.
  7. Registration at Державний реєстр речових прав (State Registry) — the notary or your lawyer registers the deed. You are the legal owner from the moment of registration.

Essential Due Diligence Checks

Your lawyer should verify all of the following before you commit to purchase:

  • Title ownership — confirm the seller is the registered owner at Державний реєстр речових прав (State Registry)
  • Encumbrances and charges — mortgages, liens, easements, or rights of way attached to the property
  • Outstanding debts — utility bills, local property taxes, service charges, or community fees in arrears
  • Planning and building permits — confirm all structures have valid building licences and are registered as built
  • Habitation certificate — confirms the property is legally habitable
  • Land use and zoning — verify the property's designated use and any restrictions on development
  • Energy performance certificate — required by law in Ukraine for all property transactions

Common Pitfalls for Foreign Buyers in Ukraine

  • Signing anything without legal advice — preliminary contracts are binding; never sign before your lawyer has reviewed
  • Using the seller's agent as your legal adviser — estate agents in Ukraine represent the seller, not you; you need independent legal representation
  • Purchasing off-plan without proper contractual protections — off-plan purchases require specific contractual guarantees; legal advice is essential
  • Underestimating transaction costs — always budget 10–12% above the agreed purchase price
  • Failing to check planning status — illegal extensions or unlicensed structures can be costly to regularise or demolish

Mortgages for Non-Residents in Ukraine

Foreign nationals can obtain mortgages in Ukraine, though terms are less favourable than for residents. Expect LTV ratios of 50%–70% and higher interest rates. A Ukraine mortgage broker and your lawyer can help navigate the process. International banks and private lenders also offer products specifically designed for foreign property purchases.
